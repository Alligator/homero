Episode Guide

SGA 5.20 "Enemy At The Gate" Episode Guide

Précis

Todd warns Atlantis about a sophisticated hive ship and encourages the team to attack the vessel before it can pose a threat to Earth.

Guide | Transcript

Transcript

TEASER

EXT—ATLANTIS, NIGHT

INT—ATLANTIS CONTROL ROOM

[Banks is at her station. Woolsey and Sheppard are nearby. The Stargate activates.]

BANKS
Incoming wormhole. Signal's coming in now.

[They look on a monitor to see a video transmission of the Wraith known as Todd standing outdoors on a planet.]

SHEPPARD
(taken aback)
Todd.
TODD
(on video)
No doubt you're surprised to see me, John Sheppard.

[Sheppard glances back at Woolsey for a moment.]

SHEPPARD
Well, you weren't looking so good last time I saw you.
TODD
Yes. Well, you'll be pleased to know the treatment was successful—although excruciatingly painful.
SHEPPARD
You're just saying that to make me feel good.
TODD
(chuckling)
I was able to return to my position as leader of the alliance, although…I have had a recent setback. Perhaps you can see what is remaining of my shuttle behind me?

[Todd steps back and in the distance the remains of a crashed Wraith ship lie in the desert.]

SHEPPARD
(sarcastic)
Yeah, nice landing.
TODD
Don't be so critical, Sheppard. It wasn't long ago my flying skills saved the lives of both you and your companions. It wasn't pilot error. I was attacked…by one of my underlings.
WOOLSEY
You'll excuse me for being blunt, but why should we care?
TODD
It isn't the fact that he attacked and at least temporarily defeated me that should concern you. It is rather…how he managed to do it.
WOOLSEY
And how was that?
TODD
Well, he acquired a rare and powerful piece of technology— one with which I believe you are quite familiar…

INT—MCKAY'S LAB

[Woolsey and Sheppard's team congregate in McKay's lab.]

MCKAY
A ZPM? Are you kidding me?
SHEPPARD
Eh, that's what he said.
RONON
I don't buy it.
WOOLSEY
Why not?
MCKAY
Well, for one thing, they don't exactly grow on trees.
RONON
He got betrayed by one of his own people. Now he wants us to help him clean up the mess. End of story.
TEYLA
It is possible that he mentioned the zero point module simply to get our attention.
WOOLSEY
Except he would also have to know that sooner or later we would discover the truth, and in the meantime, he's volunteered to place himself in our custody. Not a very good plan.
SHEPPARD
Well, you can bet there's something he's not telling us.
WOOLSEY
Granted. But that doesn't necessarily mean that he's lying about the ZPM.
MCKAY
(worried)
If this true, it could be very, very bad. I mean, inefficient power generation is the Achilles heel of Wraith technology. It's basically the reason for every technological advantage we have. If this…underling, or whatever it is that Todd calls him, is flying around in a ZPM powered hiveship, then…
WOOLSEY
It's a threat we can't tolerate. And we won't.

[He nods to Sheppard, who sighs and exchanges concerned looks with McKay.]

END TEASER

OPENING CREDITS

INT—ATLANTIS INTERROGATION ROOM

[Woolsey enters the room. Todd already sits at a table, his hands resting on top of it.]

WOOLSEY
Well…here we are again.
TODD
Indeed. The city has become quite familiar to me…almost a respite from daily life. It would be a shame to see it destroyed.
WOOLSEY
Are you suggesting that if we don't help you, that would be a likely outcome?

[Todd shrugs. Woolsey sits at the table across from him.]

WOOLSEY
All right. Let's start at the beginning. Where did the ZPM come from?
TODD
You recall I managed to acquire a few of the devices from the Replicator city before it was destroyed?
WOOLSEY
Yes, and they were lost when Colonel Sheppard took out the cloning facility.
TODD
Not all of them. I may have acquired a few more than I let on.
WOOLSEY
(shrewdly)
How many more?
TODD
Let's not get caught up in unnecessary details.
WOOLSEY
(firmly)
I like details.
TODD
Even if I gave you a number, you'd have no way of verifying it. So what's the point?
WOOLSEY
All right, then tell me this. Why now? You've had those ZPMs for over a year.
TODD
The organic nature of the hive creates certain incompatibilities with Ancient technology. I put my best scientists to work on this project, and recently one of them succeeded.
WOOLSEY
Only he didn't want to turn it over to you. He decided he wanted to keep it for himself. And now you want us to help you kick him to the curb.
TODD
(becoming serious and intense)
What you really need to know is that this hive is a work in progress. It has yet to reach its full potential, which means if you attack it now, you may be able to destroy it. But if you hesitate, it will be too late, and this hive will be unstoppable.

[Woolsey grinds his jaw.]

EXT—SPACE

[Daedalus flies through hyperspace.]

INT—DAEDALUS BRIG

[Todd sits on a cot. He is wearing a plain grey coverall. Sheppard enters. There are guards outside.]

TODD
Colonel.
SHEPPARD
Looking good.
TODD
Was it really necessary to remove my subspace transmitter and force me to wear this ridiculous outfit?
SHEPPARD
Well, after what happened last time on this ship, you can't blame us.
TODD
I take it that my request to be on the bridge when we make contact—
SHEPPARD
(interrupting)
Denied.
TODD
(nodding)
Understandable.
SHEPPARD
Here's the problem. Every time we get involved with you, I feel like I'm walking around with a live grenade in my pocket, just waiting for it to all go wrong—for that one "thing" you forgot to mention.
TODD
I assure you, in this case—
SHEPPARD
(cutting him off)
Save it. Been down this road before.

[He starts to leave.]

TODD
If you really feel that way, why would you let me go after our last encounter?
SHEPPARD
We had a deal, and you kept up your end of the bargain. And to be honest, I didn't think you'd live.
TODD
(understanding)
Ahh.
SHEPPARD
(quietly serious)
If I find out you're playing us, I'm not gonna wait for authorization. There isn't gonna be any paperwork. I'm just gonna kill you.

[Sheppard turns and leaves as Todd watches, intrigued.]

INT—DAEDALUS BRIDGE

[Sheppard's team is geared up for an away mission as the members enter the bridge and approach Caldwell.]

SHEPPARD
What's the status, Colonel?
CALDWELL
We're approaching the coordinates. You going somewhere?
SHEPPARD
Yeah. Drop us out early before we get into sensor range. We'll check it out in the jumper.
CALDWELL
That's not the plan.
MCKAY
We can go in cloaked. Stay in touch through subspace on a secure channel.
SHEPPARD
If it's what Todd says it is, you can come in guns blazing.
CALDWELL
All right. Just be careful.

[Sheppard's team leaves.]

EXT—SPACE

[The puddle jumper flies through space.]

INT—PUDDLE JUMPER

[Sheppard's team is inside. McKay studies readings on the cockpit displays.]

MCKAY
Almost in range.
SHEPPARD
Engaging cloak.

[Sheppard touches some controls, and the cloak engages.]

MCKAY
All right, getting readings now. It's a hive, all right. Hmm.
RONON
What?
MCKAY
I'm reading…a higher than normal energy output. I mean, significantly higher.
TEYLA
Consistent with the ZPM?
MCKAY
Well, it's hard to say from this far out.

INT—DAEDALUS BRIDGE

CALDWELL
(into radio)
Sheppard, what's your status?
SHEPPARD
(over radio)
McKay's getting some interesting readings, so we're going in for a closer look.

INT—PUDDLE JUMPER

[The hive ship is visible through the jumper window.]

MCKAY
I'm detecting massive energy output.
SHEPPARD
What's happening?
MCKAY
(disturbed)
It's growing.
SHEPPARD
What the hell are you talking about?
MCKAY
That's what they're doing with the surplus energy—using it to grow a larger, and if I'm reading this correctly, significantly denser outer hull. It makes sense. Normal hives have a limit of growth. I mean, any bigger or heavier and they become impossible to maneuver, let alone get into hyperspace. I mean, you make a simple left turn, and you're torn apart by your own inertia. But with a ZPM…
SHEPPARD
I get the picture. What does that mean for us?
MCKAY
When they're done, my guess is that hull will be all but impenetrable.

[The team look worried.]

SHEPPARD
All right. Well, we better take 'em out of the picture before they get that far.

[A sensor beeps rapidly.]

MCKAY
Hold on. They're…powering weapons.
RONON
They can't see us, right?
MCKAY
Of course not. They must just be running a test…

[An energy pulse heads straight for them, passing directly overhead, shaking the jumper. Everyone's eyes widen.]

MCKAY
Holy crap!
SHEPPARD
Well, what are the odds of them randomly firing a test shot directly at us!
MCKAY
I would say—given the enormity of empty space all around us—nonexistent?
SHEPPARD
(low)
They can see us.

[Sheppard steers the jumper away from the hive ship.]

MCKAY
They must've figured out a way to increase the sensitivity of their scanners.

[Another blast is a near-miss, rattling the jumper.]

MCKAY
(caustic)
Let me guess, something to do with the ZPM?
MCKAY
Probably.

INT—DAEDALUS BRIDGE

SHEPPARD
(over radio)
Daedalus, we've got a problem. Our cloak's ineffective.
CALDWELL
(into radio)
We're on our way.
(to port helmsman)
Take us in maximum sublight.
(to Marks)
Shields up.
MARKS
Yes, sir.

EXT—SPACE

[Daedalus flies forward.]

INT—PUDDLE JUMPER

[Another blast hits too close.]

SHEPPARD
I'm dropping the cloak. It ain't doing us any good.
MCKAY
Not to mention you could fire back.
SHEPPARD
(admonishing)
One thing at a time, Rodney.

EXT—SPACE

[The jumper moves evasively and dodges several pulse blasts. It gets struck in one of its nacelles and starts spinning out of control around its longitudinal axis.]

INT—PUDDLE JUMPER

MCKAY
We're hit!
SHEPPARD
I've lost controls!

[McKay jumps up and heads towards the rear compartment.]

MCKAY
I'll work out a bypass. Give me a minute.
SHEPPARD
They get a bead on us, Rodney, we're dead!

[McKay opens a panel and starts working with the crystals.]

EXT—SPACE

[The jumper continues to spin out of control as it gets close to Daedalus. Another pulse barely misses it. Further blasts are deflected by the Daedalus shields as it flies into the path of fire.]

INT—DAEDALUS BRIDGE

MARKS
It worked, sir. We've blocked the shot. Shield is holding.
CALDWELL
All right, now return fire. Main battery, full power.
MARKS
Yes, sir.

EXT—SPACE

[Daedalus fires back at the hive ship.]

INT—DAEDALUS BRIDGE

MARKS
Direct hit.
CALDWELL
Damage?
MARKS
(surprised)
Minimal.
CALDWELL
Ready missiles. We're gonna throw everything we have at this guy.
MARKS
Sir, I'm reading a massive energy buildup.

EXT—SPACE

[The hive ship sends out a large series of pulse blasts from every weapons port along its flank. All are directed at Daedalus.]

INT—DAEDALUS BRIDGE

CALDWELL
Brace for impact!

EXT—SPACE

[Several of the pulses strike the Daedalus, battering the shield at the bow.]

INT—DAEDALUS BRIDGE

[Sparks fly as the ship takes damage.]

MARKS
Shields are down! Heavy damage on all decks! We've lost hyperdrive and main weapons.
CALDWELL
Evasive maneuvers! We can't take another hit like that!

INT—PUDDLE JUMPER

[McKay stops working on the ceiling control panel.]

MCKAY
Okay, you should have flight controls now!

[Sheppard begins to move the craft.]

SHEPPARD
What about weapons?
MCKAY
Not a chance!

[Teyla has been watching the window.]

TEYLA
Something's happening.

[McKay steps forward as they all lean to look.]

EXT—SPACE

[The hive passes the Daedalus and jumps into hyperspace.]

INT—DAEDALUS GEAR UP ROOM

[Caldwell meets with Sheppard's team as they take off their tactical equipment. Outside, work teams try and repair damage to the ship.]

CALDWELL
You know, it doesn't make any sense. Most of our main systems were down. These guys had us dead to rights, and they knew it. Why would they just fly away?
TEYLA
Perhaps it's precisely because they knew we were no longer a threat.
CALDWELL
But it's not like the Wraith to be so charitable.
MCKAY
Todd said the ship isn't finished, right? They haven't adapted the full potential of the ZPM. Well, maybe the battle maxed them out.
CALDWELL
Well, if that's the case, I'd hate to meet up with them when they do finish it.

[Sheppard finishes unloading his pistol and sets it on the gear table.]

SHEPPARD
We need to get back to Atlantis. What's the prognosis on the hyperdrive?
CALDWELL
Uh, the control systems are completely shot. My guys are working on a patch, but it could be days, even weeks before we're back online. For the moment, we're not going anywhere.

[Marks enters.]

MARKS
(to Caldwell)
Sir.
CALDWELL
What is it, Marks?
MARKS
I think we may have something.

[Marks leads the way out of the room. Caldwell and Sheppard's team follow.]

INT—DAEDALUS AUXILIARY CONTROL ROOM

[Kavanaugh is amongst the crew working in the room. Caldwell and Sheppard's team enter and surround his station. He glances back, spots McKay, rolls his eyes and turns back to work.]

MCKAY
Kavanaugh? What the hell are you doing here?
KAVANAUGH
(sarcastic)
Reassignment. More fun and adventure in the Pegasus Galaxy.
CALDWELL
(irritated)
Show us what you got, please?

[Kavanaugh moves to another station. They follow.]

KAVANAUGH
It's a subspace signal. We detected it just before the hive jumped into hyperspace. It was weak. Luckily, I happened to be monitoring the sensor, or we might not have picked it up at all.
MCKAY
(studying the readout)
It's Wraith code.
KAVANAUGH
(irked)
Obviously. We're having trouble deciphering it because the message is interlaced with a residual radiation, and I've never seen it before.
MCKAY
I have. Once, when we met another me, and once when we encountered another Daedalus. This signal was sent from another reality.
CALDWELL
Uh, I'm sorry. What the hell are you talking about?
MCKAY
Some Wraith, somewhere, in another reality sent a coded signal—only whatever means they used to access subspace created a rift, allowing the signal to cross into multiple realities, including our own.
RONON
So what does it say?
MCKAY
Oh, just give me a second.

[He leans across Kavanaugh's station and starts typing. He pulls up a new readout.]

MCKAY
(sighing in despair)
Oh, great.
SHEPPARD
What is it?
MCKAY
It's a set of coordinates.
CALDWELL
Coordinates to what?
MCKAY
Earth.

[They exchange worried looks.]

INT—DAEDALUS CORRIDOR

[McKay, Sheppard, and Caldwell move through a corridor.]

SHEPPARD
You think the hive picked up the signal?
MCKAY
Why else would they leave like that? It can't be a coincidence. The Wraith know that Earth is a much richer feeding ground than anything in the Pegasus Galaxy, and they've been looking for those coordinates for years.
CALDWELL
All right. As soon as communications are back up, we'll contact Atlantis, have them relay a message to Stargate Command.
MCKAY
Wait a minute. I've got an idea. How many ships do we have available?
CALDWELL
Aside from ourselves, there's the Apollo and the Sun Tzu.
SHEPPARD
How about the Odyssey?
CALDWELL
Mmm, it's on some secret mission even I'm not supposed to know about.
MCKAY
Then two will have to be enough.
SHEPPARD
What are you suggesting?
MCKAY
Look, that hive may be super-powerful and all, but I'll bet it has to drop out of hyperspace at some point along the way for hull regeneration. I can use the sensor data from the jumpers to extrapolate exactly where that'll be.
CALDWELL
(skeptical)
Sounds pretty risky. If you're wrong, it—
MCKAY
(interrupting)
I won't be! Anyways, we can't wait until they get all the way to Earth. My guess is by that point, they'll be fully adapted to the ZPM.

[Caldwell nods.]

CALDWELL
Do it.

[McKay leaves.]

EXT—ATLANTIS, DAY

INT—ATLANTIS CONTROL ROOM

[Chuck is at his usual station. Woolsey works with another technician nearby.]

CHUCK
Sir? The Daedalus just dropped out of hyperspace.
WOOLSEY
It's about time.
CHUCK
Colonel Sheppard and his team are beaming down.

[Sheppard's team beams directly into the control room.]

WOOLSEY
Colonel. It's good to finally have you all back.
SHEPPARD
What'd we miss?
WOOLSEY
Well the good news is, there's no indication of any other Wraith ships heading for the Milky Way.
MCKAY
That's as I figured. That signal was too short and too weak. That ship only detected it because of its obvious improvements to the sensors.
SHEPPARD
All right. What's the bad news?
WOOLSEY
(to Chuck)
Show them.

[Chuck types on his keyboard, and a video of Ellis on board the Apollo appears on the screen. There are signs of ship damage behind him.]

WOOLSEY
This is part of a subspace message sent by Colonel Ellis on board the Apollo.
ELLIS
(staticy, on video)
We've engaged the hive, unfortunately, without much success. The Sun Tzu was crippled, and she's venting atmosphere. We're in the process of taking on her crew now. But unfortunately, with my engines down, I won't be able to get to the nearest Stargate for at least a month. As for the enemy, all indications are that they received very little damage and are continuing on their previous course.

[As Ellis has been speaking, McKay and Sheppard exchange worried looks.]

WOOLSEY
With those two ships out of the picture, nothing stands between that hive…and Earth.

EXT—ATLANTIS, DAY

INT—ATLANTIS CORRIDOR

[Sheppard heads down a corridor. McKay runs into him as he turns a corner.]

MCKAY
(excited)
There you are! Look, I've been thinking…
SHEPPARD
So have I.
MCKAY
There is another ship that can help defend Earth.
SHEPPARD
I know. We're standing on it.
MCKAY
The city…exactly! The only problem is…
SHEPPARD
ZPMs.
MCKAY
Yeah, but if we want to get there in time to make a difference, we need a full complement.
SHEPPARD
Where do you think I'm headed, Rodney?
MCKAY
Right.

[McKay stops short in surprise and lets Sheppard continue without him. McKay heads back the way he came.]

INT—ATLANTIS INTERROGATION ROOM

[Todd sits at the table, still in the coverall. Sheppard enters and leans across the table.]

SHEPPARD
Well, things didn't go exactly according to plan.
TODD
So I heard. You intend to hold me responsible?
SHEPPARD
All I know is that everything went sideways—the way it always does when you're involved. You mean to tell me that's a coincidence?

[Todd leans in, his hands bracing the table.]

TODD
Then you've come to make good on your threat?

[Sheppard sits in the chair opposite Todd and folds his hands, never breaking eye contact with the Wraith.]

SHEPPARD
The other ZPMs you talked to Woolsey about? Tell us where to get 'em, and maybe it'll improve your situation.
TODD
Am I supposed to be enticed by this offer?
SHEPPARD
No. I'll probably kill you anyway. But, don't forget, this Wraith betrayed you and got away with it. For no other reason, you might want to do it out of pure spite.

[Todd leans back, studying Sheppard.]

TODD
You…know how to talk to me, John Sheppard.

[Todd laughs.]

INT—ATLANTIS GATE ROOM

[Woolsey and Sheppard wait by the active Stargate. Lorne's team exits the wormhole. Lorne carries a black case.]

LORNE
We got 'em.

[He opens up the case to reveal two ZPMs inside as the wormhole shuts down.]

WOOLSEY
(incredulous)
He actually came through for us.
SHEPPARD
(dubious)
Well, maybe he's turning over a new leaf.
WOOLSEY
Get those down to Zelenka immediately.
LORNE
Yes, sir.

[Lorne starts to leave.]

SHEPPARD
Or maybe they're going to blow up when you plug them in.
WOOLSEY
Have Doctor McKay check them first.
LORNE
Right.

[Lorne's team leaves. Sheppard turns to go in the opposite direction.]

SHEPPARD
All right. I'm heading to the chair room.
WOOLSEY
Actually, Colonel, you won't be flying the city for us. In fact, you won't be coming with us at all.

[Sheppard turns back, surprised.]

SHEPPARD
What are you talking about?
WOOLSEY
You have a half an hour to pack, and then you'll be gating back to Earth. Even with the ZPMs, there's still a good chance we won't get there in time. And Earth's only other line of defense is the Ancient weapons platform. General O'Neill wants you in the chair.

[Sheppard bites his lip and nods. Woolsey puts out his hand. Sheppard shakes it.]

WOOLSEY
Good luck.
SHEPPARD
Yeah. You too.

[Sheppard heads back the other way.]

EXT—CHEYENNE MOUNTAIN, DAY

INT—SGC GATE ROOM

[Sheppard steps through the active Stargate, wearing a large backpack. Colonel Carter waits at the bottom of the ramp. She wears blue BDUs with the SG-1 patch on her right shoulder. She smiles in greeting.]

CARTER
Colonel Sheppard!
SHEPPARD
Colonel Carter!

[She pats him on the back as she leads him out of the room.]

CARTER
It's good to see you again, John.

INT—ATLANTIS CHAIR ROOM

[McKay makes some final adjustments on a control panel.]

MCKAY
(into radio)
All right. I've finished recalibration on the chair. Radek, how are things at your end?
ZELENKA
(over radio)
ZPMs are online and functioning one hundred percent. We're good to go.
MCKAY
Okay! Who's flying this puppy.

[Beckett quickly enters the room.]

BECKETT
That would be me.
MCKAY
(nonplussed)
Carson?
BECKETT
Rodney.
MCKAY
What, they called you in for this?
BECKETT
Aye.
MCKAY
You must have a higher CIA than I thought.
BECKETT
Excuse me?
MCKAY
Chair Interface Aptitude. We have a…ranking system.
BECKETT
Well, apparently, after Colonel Sheppard, I'm number two.
MCKAY
(skeptical)
Really? I gotta look into that.
BECKETT
Well, thanks for the vote of confidence, Rodney.
MCKAY
(absently)
Oh, I'm sure you'll…do fine.

[Beckett sinks into the chair.]

BECKETT
(sighing)
Great.

[He leans back in the chair, and it activates.]

BECKETT
(into radio)
I suppose I'm ready.

[He clears his throat.]

INT—ATLANTIS CONTROL ROOM

WOOLSEY
(to techs)
Raise the shield.
(into radio)
Doctor Beckett, take us out.

INT—ATLANTIS CHAIR ROOM

[As McKay worriedly watches from behind him, Beckett winces in concentration. The room shakes.]

EXT—ATLANTIS OCEAN, DAY

[The city ship of Atlantis, surrounded by its shields, rises from the water's surface.]

INT—ATLANTIS CONTROL ROOM

[The room shakes with the movement.]

INT—ATLANTIS CHAIR ROOM

[Beckett has his eyes closed in concentration.]

EXT—ATLANTIS PLANET, DAY

[The city ship rises out of the atmosphere.]

INT—SGC CORRIDOR, LEVEL 25

[Carter and Sheppard walk down the corridor.]

SHEPPARD
So, you're in command here, huh?
CARTER
Only until General Landry gets back. He's heading up a task force in Washington.

[She reaches into her pocket and pulls out her key card, swiping it at the elevator.]

CARTER
My next position is to take command of our new Daedalus-class ship.
SHEPPARD
The Phoenix?
CARTER
Actually…we're renaming it. The General Hammond.
SHEPPARD
(sympathetic)
Oh, yeah. I heard about that. Sorry. It was kinda sudden.
CARTER
(sadly)
Heart attack. I was off-world at the time.
SHEPPARD
He was a good man.
CARTER
(nodding)
He really was.

[Her expression is distant and sad. The elevator doors open, and they enter.]

SHEPPARD
So, uh, what's next? C-130 to McMurdo?
CARTER
Not exactly.

[The elevator doors close.]

INT—SGC BRIEFING ROOM

[The briefing room has been turned into an operations center with people on laptops sitting all around the table. Near the window to the Gate Room, Major Paul Davis stands by a monitor. Carter and Sheppard enter. Davis and Sheppard shake hands.]

CARTER
Colonel Sheppard, this is Major Davis. Homeworld Security.
SHEPPARD
Major.
DAVIS
Colonel.
SHEPPARD
What are we looking at?

[Davis gestures to the map on the computer screen. It shows Earth surrounded by concentric range rings, and the moon with a small red dot moving around it.]

DAVIS
It's the hive ship. It dropped out of hyperspace and established orbit around the moon just before you arrived.
SHEPPARD
You've got to be kidding me! It's not supposed to be here for weeks!
CARTER
Evidently, this is no ordinary hive ship.
SHEPPARD
(ruefully)
Well, that much I know. What's it doing?
DAVIS
For the moment, nothing.

[The screen shows the hive orbiting the moon.]

CARTER
Best we can guess is it's completing it's ZPM upgrades prior to launching an attack.
SHEPPARD
Then we got to make to make a move first. You got any F-302s?
DAVIS
We have a squadron on permanent stand-by with Earth Defense, but those birds aren't outfitted for anything more than fighter-interceptor duties. There's no way they're gonna take on a hive…
SHEPPARD
How long to fit 'em with nukes?
DAVIS
(looking to Carter)
Well, that could take some doing.

[Carter nods.]

DAVIS
I'll get right on it.

[He leaves.]

SHEPPARD
(to Carter)
These pilots have any experience fighting the Wraith?
CARTER
(admonishing)
John, that's not why I brought you here.
SHEPPARD
I can sit in the chair and wait for the Wraith to get stronger, or I can take the fight to them. What do you want to do?

[Carter sighs, troubled.]

EXT—SPACE

[The city-ship of Atlantis flies through hyperspace.]

INT—WOOLSEY'S OFFICE

[Woolsey sits at his desk. Teyla and Ronon arrive at the door.]

TEYLA
Mister Woolsey. You wanted to see us?
WOOLSEY

Sorry about that.

EXT—PACIFIC OCEAN AIRSPACE, DAY

[Atlantis flies towards the ocean, its shield still visible.]

INT—ATLANTIS CONTROL ROOM

[The smoke is clearing. Earth daylight shines from the Gate room windows.]

WOOLSEY
We've completed our reentry and as far as I can tell, we're still in one piece. Doctor Beckett thinks he can bring us in over water, but…you might want to alert the Navy. It's gonna be close.

INT—SGC BRIEFING ROOM

[Carter signals Davis with a pat on the shoulder.]

CARTER
(into radio)
Understood.
DAVIS
We are tracking them again.

[He turns to where Carter is already looking at the large monitor. It shows Atlantis has travelled from the east coast of Asia to its current position northwest of Hawaii.]

DAVIS
We should be able to project coordinates for splashdown.
CARTER
(to Harriman)
Walter, you'd better get me the President. It looks like Atlantis is coming home.

[Carter smiles, her eyes glistening.]

INT—ATLANTIS INFIRMARY

[Ronon lies in an infirmary bed wearing scrubs. Sheppard leans on the footrest.]

SHEPPARD
Well, how you feelin?
RONON
I'm ready to get out of here.
SHEPPARD
Take your time. The city's pretty banged up. Even with the ZPMs, we're not goin' anywhere for a while.
RONON
And when they fix it? Do you really think they're going to let us take it back to Pegasus?
SHEPPARD
I'll make sure you get home.
RONON
This is my home.

[Banks arrives, her hair down and dressed in civilian clothing.]

BANKS
Am I interrupting?

[Sheppard exchanges looks with Ronon, who smiles at him. Sheppard looks back at Banks.]

SHEPPARD
Uh…no, uh…I was just leaving.

[Sheppard leaves. Banks approaches Ronon's bed. Ronon smiles at Banks and tries to sit up better.]

BANKS
(nervously)
Hi…I heard you…died…and came back to life.
RONON
Pretty much. There's, uh, there's a few things I still need to do.
BANKS
(smiling)
You up for taking a walk?
RONON
I don't see why not.
BANKS
Good. There's something you should see.

[She moves to help him out of bed.]

INT—ATLANTIS INTERROGATION ROOM

[Sheppard enters. Todd stands by the table. Sheppard crosses his arms.]

TODD
So, all is well! Your homeworld was saved, you and your friends survived, and all thanks to my zero point modules.
SHEPPARD
That's right. The question is, what would happen next time?
TODD
(warily)
Is there going to be a next time?

[Sheppard studies him.]

EXT—ATLANTIS BALCONY, DAY

[Banks helps Ronon out to the balcony. Keller, McKay, Teyla, Woolsey, and Beckett are already there.]

BANKS
You have room for two more?
WOOLSEY
Of course!

[Teyla rubs Ronon's shoulder comfortingly as he steps up next to her.]

WOOLSEY
Ronon, welcome to Earth.

[They all stare at the Golden Gate Bridge of San Francisco, California and into the bay beyond it.]

KELLER
(cautiously)
No one can see us, right?
WOOLSEY
We managed to activate the cloak before we came in too close. This entire area is under naval quarantine.

[Sheppard arrives and stands between Teyla and Ronon.]

SHEPPARD
Oh, so this is where everyone went, huh?

[Keller nudges McKay, who's looking at the view with a pensive expression.]

KELLER
You okay?
MCKAY
(drily)
I'm alive.

[Keller snickers. McKay puts his arm around her, pulling her close.]

MCKAY
And I've got you. What else do I need?
SHEPPARD
Nice view.
WOOLSEY
Yes. Yes, it is.

FADE OUT

END CREDITS
