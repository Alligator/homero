SGA 3.01 "No Man's Land Part 2" Episode Guide

Précis

Sheppard's ship vanishes during a space melee against the Wraith. Meanwhile, Weir is recalled to Stargate Command as Col. Caldwell leads a mission to keep the Wraith from reaching Earth.

[The move off.]

INT—WRAITH HIVE SHIP CORRIDOR

[Two Wraith soldiers drag a semi-conscious Sheppard down a corridor, lead by a Wraith. They encounter Michael.]

WRAITH
You should not be here.
MICHAEL
She asked me to question the prisoner…If you'd prefer to discuss it with her…
WRAITH
Release him.

[The soldiers push Sheppard to Michael. He walks on his own, touching a wound on his face.]

MICHAEL
I will take him from here.

[Michael and Sheppard walk away together. Michael lowers the gun he'd been holding on Sheppard.]

SHEPPARD
You had me worried there.
MICHAEL
You've slowed them down at least for a little while. We need to get to a transport ship and hope your friends find us.
SHEPPARD
Not 'til we free Ronon and McKay.
MICHAEL
We're headed toward them now.

[Michael hand a handgun to Sheppard.]

SHEPPARD
Now that's what I call help.

EXT—HYPERSPACE

[Daedalus moves through hyperspace.]

INT—DAEDALUS BRIDGE

KLEINMAN
Exiting hyperspace in ten seconds.
CALDWELL
Launch the moment the target is acquired. Don't wait for my order.
KLEINMAN
Five, four, three…

EXT—SPACE

[Many Darts fly outside the two Hives. Daedalus exits hyperspace right in front of them. It launches its missiles instantly.]

INT—DAEDALUS BRIDGE

KLEINMAN
Missiles are away.
CALDWELL
Dart activity?
KLEINMAN
Quite a number of them, sir. They're moving to intercept our missiles.
CALDWELL
(to himself)
Now would be a good time, Orion.

EXT—SPACE

[Many of the missiles explode far from the Hives as the Darts sacrifice themselves. One finally impacts a Hive.]

INT—WRAITH HIVE SHIP CORRIDOR

[McKay and Ronon are shaken by the blast.]

MCKAY
What the hell?

INT—WRAITH HIVE SHIP CORRIDOR

[Michael approaches a control station.]

MICHAEL
One of your ships has launched an attack. Their first strike inflicted serious damage on this hive.
SHEPPARD
Good.
MICHAEL
The other hive was undamaged, but your vessel is continuing to engage.
SHEPPARD
Are you going to go through the whole play-by-play, or are we going to get out of here?
MICHAEL
This way.

INT—WRAITH HIVE SHIP CORRIDOR

[Many soldiers run past Ronon and McKay's hiding place.]

RONON
This ship's engaged in battle.
MCKAY
With who?
RONON
Doesn't matter.
MCKAY
Well, it matters to us.
RONON
Why?
MCKAY
Because if it's our guys, then our chances of survival just went back above zero, albeit fractionally.
RONON
If it's our guys, all the more reason to damage this ship and help them win the battle. Now, move it.

INT—DAEDALUS BRIDGE

KLEINMAN
One missile got through, sir. One of the Wraith ships suffered serious damage, but the other one is— Sir, the Orion has arrived. The other hive is moving to intercept.
CALDWELL
They launched their weapons?
KLEINMAN
Negative, sir.
CALDWELL
Open a secure channel.
(into radio)
Major Lorne, now would be a good time to open fire.

EXT—SPACE

[The Hives are firing on both ships.]

INT—ORION BRIDGE

LORNE
Yes, sir. I was just thinking the same thing. I gave the order, but nothing happened. Zelenka!
ZELENKA
I'm trying! speaks Czech.
LORNE
We're having a little difficulty transferring power from shields to weapons.

INT—DAEDALUS BRIDGE

CALDWELL
There's a hive bearing down on your position.
LORNE
Yes, sir, I see that.

INT—ORION BRIDGE

LORNE
Stand by. Doc, you're killing us, here.
ZELENKA
Okay. I have it. I have it. I have it.
LORNE
Firing drones!

EXT—SPACE

[The Orion launches dozens of drones at the Hives, as it takes heavy fire itself. The drones evade the Darts and make their way to one of the Hives, where they inflict such heavy damage that it explodes.]

INT—DAEDALUS BRIDGE

KLEINMAN
Sir, the remaining hive is shifting their attack to the Orion.
CALDWELL
Move to intercept.
KLEINMAN
Orion has no shields. We're not going to make it there in time, sir.

[Crewmen try to contain the damage on the bridge.]

INT—WRAITH HIVE SHIP CORRIDOR

[Sheppard et al are on the surviving Hive. They find the cocoons where Ronon and McKay had been, empty.]

SHEPPARD
Where are they?
MICHAEL
I'm sorry. They must have been taken for feeding.
SHEPPARD
No, no. These were cut from the inside. They escaped.
MICHAEL
They could be anywhere on this ship.
SHEPPARD
Well, that's a start.
MICHAEL
Why? What could their objective possibly be?
SHEPPARD
If I know Ronon, they're somewhere where they can do as much damage as they can, and McKay would know where and how to do that, so…
MICHAEL
There's a control station this way.

EXT—SPACE

[The remaining Hive fires on both Daedalus and Orion, with more force directed at Orion.]

INT—ORION BRIDGE

[The bridge is smoky and sparking.]

LORNE
One more shot, Zelenka! That's all I'm asking!
ZELENKA
I just cannot give you what I do not have! There's too much damage! We need to abandon ship!

[He starts to leave the bridge.]

LORNE
Hey, Doc! Listen to me!
ZELENKA
We sacrificed our shield capability for that first salvo. This ship is going to blow any moment, Major.
LORNE
(into radio)
Colonel Caldwell, I'm afraid we've done everything we can on our end.
We request immediate beam-out to Daedalus.

INT—DAEDALUS BRIDGE

CALDWELL
Get them over here!

INT—WRAITH HIVE SHIP CORRIDOR

[McKay works at a control station as Ronon keeps a look-out.]

MCKAY
Their operating system is a mess. Thank goodness I remembered DOS.
Trust me, that was hilarious.
RONON
Have you done it yet?
MCKAY
Look, this ship has been seriously damaged. It's hard for me to find something to overload.
SHEPPARD
Let Mikey try it.

[The two pairs see each other. Ronon whips out a knife and points it at Michael.]

SHEPPARD
Whoa, whoa, whoa, whoa. He's helping us.
MCKAY
Where did you come from?
SHEPPARD
I managed to latch on to the hull of the hive before it went into hyperspace.
RONON
Nice move.
SHEPPARD
Saw it in a movie once.

[The ship shudders under attack.]

SHEPPARD
We've got to get to the Daedalus.
MCKAY
The Daedalus—the Daedalus is here?
MICHAEL
First we need to get to the transport.
RONON
Why should I trust you?
MICHAEL
Because I'm trusting you.

[Michael hands his weapon to Ronon then steps away. Ronon points the weapon at Michael. Sheppard grabs his arm.]

SHEPPARD
Because I said so.

[They move off. Ronon grabs Sheppard so that Michael can't hear them. Rodney stands with them.]

RONON
I don't like this.
SHEPPARD
You got a better idea?
RONON
We take the ship on our own and leave him here.
SHEPPARD
He's the reason I'm alive right now.
MCKAY
Besides, I don't think that's even possible. It took me days to crack the Wraith dart interface, and this ship is a whole lot bigger.
RONON
I don't like it.
SHEPPARD
Yeah, I get it, you hate the Wraith.
RONON
Him in particular.
SHEPPARD
(to McKay)
Is there anything you can do to boost the radio signal? I want to reach the Daedalus as soon as possible.
MCKAY
Well, I could try.
MICHAEL
Hang on. They'll most likely open fire on us the second we leave the bay.
MCKAY
Oh, great. The day just gets better and better.

EXT—SPACE

[A single transport ship leaves the Wraith ship and speeds toward Daedalus. The Orion explodes.]

INT—DAEDALUS BRIDGE

[Through the damage and chaos, Lorne makes his way to Caldwell.]

LORNE
Sorry, sir. We did our best.
CALDWELL
Nothing to apologize for, Major.
(to Kleinman)
Bring all rail guns to bear on the main Dart bays. Hold fire till I give the order. Channel every ounce of power to the forward shield until then.
KLEINMAN
I have a contact, sir. A single Wraith ship, possibly a transport, just left the hive and is on a course for us. It's broadcasting a comm on our frequencies.
CALDWELL
What the hell do they want?
ZELENKA
Maybe they want to surrender.
CALDWELL
Not likely. Let's hear it.
SHEPPARD
(over radio)
This is Colonel John Sheppard, authentication code Alpha Seven Tango Three. Please respond.
CALDWELL
Open a channel. Colonel Sheppard, we'd written you off.
SHEPPARD
Don't get all emotional on me now. Look, I've got McKay, Ronon, and Michael aboard a Wraith transport.
CALDWELL
Michael?
SHEPPARD
Yes, sir, and he's got a plan I think you're gonna like. But it probably won't be long before they start shooting at us.
KLEINMAN
The hive just opened fire on the transport, sir.
CALDWELL
Beam them directly to the bridge.

[Sheppard, McKay, Ronon, and Michael beam in. Sheppard turns to Caldwell.]

SHEPPARD
Colonel!
CALDWELL
Colonel. You said something about a plan?
MICHAEL
I've disabled their jamming code, but it's only a matter of time before they realize.
SHEPPARD
All you have to do is send over a nuke.
CALDWELL
We deployed all our warheads in the first attack.
KLEINMAN
The hive is launching darts, sir.
CALDWELL
Open up on those bays, all batteries!

EXT—SPACE

[The two ships fire on each other furiously. The Wraith Hive is hit on its broadsides, and the repeated hits being to damage it, causing explosions. Daedalus approaches while firing. It takes severe hits on its underside.]

INT—DAEDALUS BRIDGE

[The ship takes major damage. The gunner is thrown from his seat. A fire starts behind Caldwell. Sheppard takes over the weapons position. McKay rushes to the back.]

CALDWELL
Cap that off! Report!
KLEINMAN
The plan worked, sir. The concentrated fire on the bays created a number of secondary explosions. They've stopped firing.
SHEPPARD
Nice work, Colonel.
CALDWELL
Fortunately a draw is a win for our side. Ship's status?
DAEDALUS
Shields completely depleted, and we're venting atmosphere.
CALDWELL
Seal off all affected decks.
DAEDALUS
Yes, sir, but that last blast took out our life support, and the back-ups aren't responding. As of right now, we're shallow-breathing.
RONON
Meaning?
MCKAY
Meaning that in a little under nine hours, we're all going to suffocate.

INT—DAEDALUS CORRIDOR

[Zelenka and McKay walk with Kleinman.]

ZELENKA
As l—As long as we don't have to worry about shields and weapons, we may be able to cannibalize parts from some of our other systems, in order to create, at the very least, a-a CO2 filtration system.
MCKAY
By the time we got something like that up and running, the CO2 levels will overwhelm any makeshift scrubbers we'd be able to concoct.
ZELENKA
But, it's worth a try.
MCKAY
Yes, yeah, but regardless, we need more electricians and welders than are technically certified on the ship's roster. Anyone, and I mean anyone, who took shop needs to report to us.
KLEINMAN
There are teams assembling on every deck.
MCKAY
Good, good.

[The arrive at a supply room and see sets of personal survival packs.</blockquote>

MCKAY
Ah, now we're talking. How many more of these do we have?
KLEINMAN
Twenty in total.
MCKAY
How many people on board?
KLEINMAN
In and around the two hundred mark.
MCKAY
Oh, so, I guess buddy-breathing our way through this one's out of the question.
KLEINMAN
I'm afraid so, sir, yes.
MCKAY
Okay, fine they're reserved for people working on getting life-support systems operational. We need those people as clear-headed as possible.
KLEINMAN
And by "those people"…
MCKAY
Yes, I will be one of them.
ZELENKA
How many levels are inaccessible?
KLEINMAN
Seven.
ZELENKA
Okay, we need to find out if there are any pockets of breathable atmosphere in there. We may be able to squeeze another hour or so out of regions that have been sealed off.
MCKAY
We can't exactly transfer the Oh-two where we need it.
ZELENKA
Yes, but we could be—
MCKAY
We will be able to access key panels that have been closed off to us. Let's get Sheppard in a 302. He can eyeball it from the outside. I'll take this.

[He takes one of the packs.]

EXT—SPACE

[Sheppard flies an F302 around the ship, shining a light on it to see the damage and atmosphere escaping from many places.]

INT—F302 COCKPIT

SHEPPARD
These sections are completely open to space Rodney. There aren't even pockets you could beam into. What about an E.V.A.?

INT—DAEDALUS POWER ROOM

[Rodney works on panels filled with crystals.]

MCKAY
The space suits were stored in those sections.
SHEPPARD
(over radio)
All right. I'm heading back.
MCKAY
Copy that.
ZELENKA
Have you tried routing the power through the initial—
MCKAY
Yes, yes, yes. We have tried everything.
ZELENKA
Subspace communications?
MCKAY
(shakily)
We are floating between two galaxies, Radek. Atlantis has no ship to send, and even if we were somehow able to repair the array and get a message back to Earth, it would still take them weeks to get a rescue here.
ZELENKA
I wasn't thinking of rescue. I was thinking simply of telling them that we've stopped the Wraith, and perhaps letting someone know the sacrifice that we've done.
MCKAY
Well, a noble sentiment, but I would prefer to dedicate my last breath of air to getting more air.
(inspired)
Wait a second.

INT—DAEDALUS BRIEFING ROOM

[McKay speaks to Caldwell, Michael and Sheppard.]

MCKAY
The only breathable air within light years is on the Wraith ship.
CALDWELL
There're also Wraith aboard that Wraith ship, doctor.
SHEPPARD
So we take them. Beam an assault team in.
MICHAEL
You would be far out-numbered.
SHEPPARD
Well, we're dead here. I'll take my chances.
MICHAEL
I admire your courage, Colonel, but you would be dead there as surely as here… Unless we send over the gas.
MCKAY
Yes, yes, yes. That could work.
MICHAEL
The retrovirus gas would make them virtually harmless.
SHEPPARD
Do we still have it on board?
CALDWELL
Yes, we do. And now that Michael's disabled their jamming codes…
MCKAY
We finish the plan we started.
SHEPPARD
We beam over the gas and wait. We wait long enough, they won't even want to put up a fight.
MCKAY
Because they'll be humans with no memories. I can't believe I might actually survive this.
CALDWELL
We're almost out of breathable air now, and the retrovirus takes a minimum ten hours to run its course.
SHEPPARD
Yeah, we'll rotate the crew in and out of the 302s. (to Michael)
If this works, can you fly the hive ship?
MICHAEL
I could.
SHEPPARD
What do you say, Colonel?
CALDWELL
(into radio)
Weaps, get us within beaming range of the hive.
WEAPONS
Yes, sir.

EXT—CHEYENNE MOUNTAIN COMPLEX, NIGHT

INT—SGC VISITORS' OFFICE

[Landry knocks on the doorway of Weir's SGC office. The door is open.]

WEIR
(concerned)
Anything?
LANDRY
No. No contacts yet. Our tests of the Mark Two generator and the chair in Antarctica are promising.
WEIR
"Promising." That doesn't sound very promising.
LANDRY
Yeah, we're fairly confident the Ancient weapon will take care of the hive ships, but doing so will probably require the last of our drones, and there goes our secret weapon. We'd really rather not have to do that. How'd it go with the I.O.A.?
WEIR
Oh, you know. I mean does anyone ever come out of an I.O.A. deposition saying, "That went well"?
LANDRY
No, I don't suppose they do.
WEIR
It was what it was.
LANDRY
Well, at least most of the time you're a galaxy away. That Woolsey guy is here almost every other week.

[Landry sits across from Weird.]

WEIR
I think they're going to ask me to step down.
LANDRY
Well, if they're just going to ask, you can always say no.
WEIR
I messed this one up, General.
LANDRY
Yeah, you did.
WEIR
And I lost some of the best people I've ever—
LANDRY
But you proceeded with the blessing of the I.O.A and Stargate Command, as I recall.
WEIR
Somehow, that doesn't seem to help much.
LANDRY
"Success is not final. Failure is not fatal. It's the courage to continue that counts." And I think you've got that in spades.
WEIR
Churchill?
LANDRY
Yeah except for that last part about you. That was all me. Get some sleep, Elizabeth.
WEIR
Yes, sir.

[Landry leaves, and Weir contemplates his words.]

EXT—SPACE

[Daedalus moves slowly around the Wraith ship, the Milky Way visible behind it.]

INT—DAEDALUS CORRIDOR OUTSIDE BRIDGE

[Crewmen sit on the floor as those with survival breathing packs work on the ship. McKay is one of those working. Sweating, he walks slowly on the bridge.]

INT—DAEDALUS BRIDGE

KLEINMAN
(weakly)
Our CO2 levels are getting dangerous, sir.
CALDWELL
(weakly)
The gas has barely had a chance to run its course.

[Sheppard and a team of marines sit behind Caldwell. They stand.]

SHEPPARD
We don't have much of a choice. We've got to go now.

[They leave the bridge, Ronon assisting McKay.]

INT—WRAITH HIVE SHIP CORRIDOR

[The team beams aboard. The move out and find many Wraith now turned human, lying on the floor. One gasps. Sheppard looks on over.]

SHEPPARD
Some of them are dead.
LORNE
Looks like they killed each other.
WRAITH TURNED HUMAN
What's happened?

[Sheppard and the others turn their weapons on the man for a moment.]

SHEPPARD
It's all right.
(to a marine)
Find a holding area. Start marshaling the survivors there.
MCKAY
The queen's chamber should be that way.

INT—QUEEN'S CHAMBER ON HIVE SHIP

[They arrive cautiously. The Queen struggles on her throne, her face hidden.]

SHEPPARD
Hello? You all right?

[Lorne goes to her aid.]

LORNE
It's okay. You're going to be all right now.

[The Queen, still a Wraith, grabs Lorne at the throat and lifts him off the deck. She yells and prepares to feed on him. McKay and the others shoot her, and she drops Lorne on the stairs.]

LORNE
It didn't work on her.
MCKAY
Oh. Beckett wondered if the females would be immune.
LORNE
Yeah. Something you might have mentioned before she almost killed me, Rodney.
MCKAY
I only thought of it now.
MARINE
(over radio)
Colonel Sheppard, so far we haven't run into a single Wraith, sir.
SHEPPARD
Understood.
(into radio)
Sheppard to Daedalus.
CALDWELL
(over radio)
Go ahead.
SHEPPARD
(into radio)
You can start sending people over, sir. They're all human, and the air is good. I think we're out of the woods.

TO BE CONTINUED

FADE OUT
