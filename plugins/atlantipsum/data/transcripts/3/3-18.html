The jumper's sensors should be able to locate them.

[Zelenka continues to work.]

INT—PUDDLE JUMPER BAY

[Sheppard cautiously approaches, P-90 at ready. He spots the water near the door to the jumper.]

INT—PUDDLE JUMPER

[Sheppard heads in, seeing Graydon sitting in the pilot's chair.]

SHEPPARD
Graydon?

[He turns the chair around. Graydon's corpse is a dehydrated husk.]

SHEPPARD
(into radio)
Graydon's dead. Fed upon.

INT—DRILLING STATION CREW QUARTERS

WEIR
(into radio)
What about Dickenson?

INT—PUDDLE JUMPER

SHEPPARD
(into radio)
There's no sign of him yet. I'll see if we can find him using the, uh…

INT—DRILLING STATION CREW QUARTERS

[Weir and Teyla exchange worried looks.]

WEIR
(into radio)
Sheppard? Sheppard, come in!

INT—PUDDLE JUMPER

[Sheppard senses someone behind him. He spins, raising his weapon to find the queen standing in the back of the jumper. She hisses. He tries to fire, but her mind control paralyzes him. He groans in resistance, but cannot stop his hands from dropping his weapon, letting it fall against his chest, still held by the strap.]

WRAITH QUEEN
You are Sheppard. The one who can fly this ship. You will return me to the surface. Kneel.

[Resisting, Sheppard is forced to kneel.]

MCKAY
(over radio)
Sheppard, this is McKay. What have you got? Sheppard, can you hear me?

INT—DRILLING PLATFORM CONTROL ROOM

[Zelenka and McKay exchange worried looks.]

MCKAY
(into radio, worried)
Is something wrong?

INT—DRILLING PLATFORM CREW QUARTERS

[Weir and Teyla look worried as well.]

INT—PUDDLE JUMPER

WRAITH QUEEN
You will fly me to the Stargate and return me to my people.

[Sheppard shakes, trying to resist. Ronon appears in the doorway, pistol aimed. Sheppard notices him. The queen sees Sheppard's diverted attention and spins as Ronon fires. His blast shatters the window to the jumper, which is immersed in the ocean. Water pours into the jumper, washing out Sheppard and the queen.]

INT—DRILLING PLATFORM CORRIDOR

[Sheppard awakens in a corridor. He is soaked, and the floor is wet.]

MCKAY
(over radio, panicking)
Sheppard, this is McKay. Please respond! Sheppard, come in.
SHEPPARD
(weakly, into radio)
I'm here.

INT—DRILLING PLATFORM CONTROL ROOM

[Zelenka sighs in relief.]

ZELENKA
(into radio)
We had to throw the emergency force fields up. The section you were in was flooding.

INT—DRILLING PLATFORM CORRIDOR

[The conversation switches between that location and the control room.]

SHEPPARD
I noticed.
MCKAY
You were thrown by the force of the water clear of the force field bulkhead.

[Sheppard spots Ronon lying nearby.]

SHEPPARD
Ronon?

[Ronon groans and stands. He walks over to the forcefield and touches it. The other side of the corridor is completely filled with water.]

RONON
I missed.
SHEPPARD
I noticed that, too.
MCKAY
Everyone else is accounted for. Any sign of Dickenson?

[Sheppard stares down the corridor in concern.]

SHEPPARD
Stand by, McKay.

[The queen lies unconscious further down the corridor.]

MCKAY
Look, I don't know what just happened, but we are now cut off from the jumper.

[Ronon aims his gun at point blank range from the Wraith queen.]

RONON
I won't miss now.
SHEPPARD
Wait.
RONON
What for?
SHEPPARD
Well, we lost the jumper and we lost communications with Atlantis. Maybe she can help us get out of this place.

EXT—DRILLING PLATFORM

INT—DRILLING PLATFORM CHAMBER

[The queen awakens to find herself strapped down to a gurney with an i.v. in her arm. Weir, Sheppard, and Ronon stand nearby.]

WEIR
Welcome back. I trust you're comfortable?
SHEPPARD
That groggy sensation you're feeling are the sedatives we're pumping you full of. Just a precaution. As for the other precautions…

[Ronon sneers as he flourishes his weapon, changing the setting.]

WEIR
Now, you're going to tell us everything we want to know.
WRAITH QUEEN
In exchange for?
WEIR
(firm)
This is not a negotiation.
WRAITH QUEEN
I disagree.
RONON
Let me get this over with.

[Ronon aims his gun. The queen sits up, hissing and pulling against the restraints. Ronon shoves her back down as Weir backs up. They all take a moment.]

WEIR
How did you get aboard this station? Are there any more of you?

[The queen hisses. Ronon shifts his position with his gun.]

WRAITH QUEEN
You are all about to die.
WEIR
(drily)
Really?
SHEPPARD
You care to elaborate on that at all?

[The queen sighs and turns away.]

EXT—DRILLING PLATFORM

INT—DRILLING PLATFORM CONTROL ROOM

[Zelenka walks over to McKay.]

ZELENKA
We should be able to keep the force fields up until Atlantis sends a rescue jumper, but station communications are still down, so…
MCKAY
I should have listened to Teyla. This wouldn't have happened.
ZELENKA
Don't worry about it, Rodney, it's not your fault.
MCKAY
I'm the one who sent those men back to the jumper.
ZELENKA
Well, the sensors indicated that there was nobody here, so…
COLEMAN
I don't think there was, sir. I think the Wraith arrived here after we did the initial scans.
MCKAY
What?
COLEMAN
Well, you asked me to do a search with the remote sensors. I found something. We didn't notice it on the way down because it's radiating very little E.M.

[She brings up a display on the monitor.]

MCKAY
(into radio)
Guys, head back here. I think I have a few answers.

[Later, Sheppard and Weir have joined the others.]

SHEPPARD
A Wraith cruiser?
MCKAY
It seems completely buried in silt.
ZELENKA
Which means it's been here a very, very, very long time.
MCKAY
Yes, give or take a "very."
SHEPPARD
That doesn't explain how she got here.
WEIR
Maybe she swam over.
SHEPPARD
No, no. The pressure at that depth is—
MCKAY
(interrupting)
Is deadly to humans, but she's not human, remember?
ZELENKA
Yes, it's difficult to imagine, but with a Wraith's ability to heal itself…

[They exchange worried looks.]

INT—DRILLING PLATFORM CORRIDOR

[Sheppard and Weir brief Teyla.]

TEYLA
And there are no other Wraith on board?
SHEPPARD
No way to know for sure. Our prisoner isn't talking. Other than: "You're all about to die."
WEIR
Which may or may not be an empty threat.
TEYLA
And you have no way of knowing?
SHEPPARD
Well, she said it with a lot of confidence.
TEYLA
There is a way to find out.
SHEPPARD
We've already been down that road.
WEIR
And what is to stop her asserting control over you again?
TEYLA
She is heavily sedated, and this time, I know exactly what I am dealing with.

INT—DRILLING STATION CHAMBER

[Weir, Sheppard, and Teyla enter the room where Ronon's been guarding the queen.]

WRAITH QUEEN
(spotting Teyla)
You…?
TEYLA
I was caught off-guard the first time. That will not happen again.
WRAITH QUEEN
It does not matter.
SHEPPARD
Well, we think it does.
WRAITH QUEEN
Your fate is sealed.
TEYLA
(to Ronon)
If you have any reason to suspect that I am not myself, do not hesitate to use that.
RONON
I won't.

[Sheppard and Weir exchange concerned looks as Teyla goes into a meditative state.]

TEYLA
I see her…

FLASHBACK

INT—WRAITH HIVE SHIP

[The queen stares from her window as several hives float above the planet of Atlantis.]

TEYLA
(voiceover)
…leading the first wave of ships that attacked Atlantis during the war.

END FLASHBACK

TEYLA
(continuing)
Queen of a great alliance. Her ship was damaged during the battle. It crashed into the ocean.

[The queen hisses and struggles to resist.]

TEYLA
She fed upon her own crew to survive. Too deep to ever swim to the surface. Waking…between centuries…waiting for rescue that never came…until finally she was alone.

FLASHBACK

[The queen awakens from hibernation.]

TEYLA
(continuing, voiceover)
She sensed our presence during our descent. She sensed…me. And saw us as her one chance at freedom from her prison.

[The queen walks down the corridors of her ship, then swims to the drilling platform.]

END FLASHBACK

TEYLA
The water was…crushing, but she was determined. Finally, after all these millennia…a ship!

FLASHBACK

INT—PUDDLE JUMPER

[The queen tries to start the puddle jumper.]

TEYLA
(voiceover)
But she did not possess the gene to fly it.

[The queen hisses her frustration.]

'END FLASHBACK

SHEPPARD
She used Dickenson to call for me.

FLASHBACK

INT—HIVE SHIP

[The queen works the controls of her ship.]

END FLASHBACK

[Teyla breathes heavily, half collapsing.]

WEIR
Teyla?

TEYLA/WRAITH QUEEN You are all about to die.</blockquote>

SHEPPARD
That does not sound like Teyla.
TEYLA
You are all about to die! You are all about to die!

[Sheppard and Ronon aim their guns.]

SHEPPARD
Teyla, snap out of it!
TEYLA
Wait!

'FLASHBACK

[An alarm pulses on the hive ship.]

END FLASHBACK

TEYLA
She has activated the self-destruct device of the Wraith cruiser. It is set to detonate within two hours.

[The queen sighs, looking triumphant.]

SHEPPARD
So…not an empty threat.

[The team exchange worried looks.]

INT—DRILLING PLATFORM CONTROL ROOM

[Sheppard and Weir enter.]

MCKAY
How's Teyla?
WEIR
She's resting. I think it took a lot more out of her than she's willing to admit.
SHEPPARD
What's the news?
MCKAY
Okay, well, it looks like the Ancients chose this section of the ocean floor because the planet's crust is remarkably thin right below us, allowing them to drill directly into the magma and maximize the geothermal power output.
SHEPPARD
Okay.
ZELENKA
Which means there's a great deal of potential energy directly beneath this station.
SHEPPARD
That's the news? That this place works as advertised?
MCKAY
No, no, the news is that if they were to detonate, say, the self-destruct of a Wraith cruiser on just such a wafer-thin crust—
WEIR
(realizing)
Oh my God.
ZELENKA
All that thermal energy directly beneath us will be released all at once.
SHEPPARD
So…bad news.
MCKAY
Yeah, the initial explosion will be magnified a thousand times. We're talking catastrophic.
SHEPPARD
So it won't just be us that's affected.
ZELENKA
No, Atlantis will be affected as well. It's well within the blast radius.
WEIR
And we have no way to warn them?
MCKAY
They'd never even see it coming.
SHEPPARD
So we, uh, disarm the self-destruct.
MCKAY
That's brilliant, but we still need to get there.
WEIR
And how do we do that without the jumper?
SHEPPARD
We're way behind schedule. Uh, they're going to send a rescue jumper any minute now.
MCKAY
Even if they've left already, I mean, the moment we were overdue…
ZELENKA
Yeah, they won't make it in time.
SHEPPARD
How far is the cruiser?
ZELENKA
Um… Less than a kilometer.

[They look at the display.]

SHEPPARD
She swam that far? What is she?
ZELENKA
A formidable enemy.
MCKAY
(inspired)
I have an idea.

INT—DRILLING PLATFORM POOL ROOM

[Sheppard, Weir, and McKay enter. McKay walks over to where some pressure suits are hanging by the wall.]

MCKAY
I found these in the database when we first got here. They were used primarily to make repairs to the exterior of the station.
SHEPPARD
Very cool.
MCKAY
Yeah, you should be able to traverse the distance between here and the, uh, cruiser.
SHEPPARD
You mean "we."
MCKAY
Uh, yes, of course. We.

[Weir walks over to the pool.]

WEIR
This must be how she got in.
SHEPPARD
Probably. All right, let's get suited up.
MCKAY
Right.

EXT—ATLANTIS OCEAN

[Sheppard and McKay walk from the drilling station to the hive ship in pressure suits. McKay is hyperventilating inside his suit.]

SHEPPARD
You're breathing too hard.
MCKAY
Forgive me for being aware of exactly how much pressure is being exerted on this suit right now.
SHEPPARD
Just think of it as a walk on the beach…a beach that's about to explode.
MCKAY
That's supposed to make me feel better?
SHEPPARD
No, it's supposed to make you walk faster. We're on the clock here.
WEIR
(over radio)
This is Weir. How close are you?
SHEPPARD
(into radio)
Almost there, but we're running out of time. We may need Teyla to do her Kreskin act again.

INT—DRILLING PLATFORM CORRIDOR

[Weir glances over at Teyla.]

WEIR
I'd rather not have to. I think it could be much too dangerous for her.

EXT—ATLANTIS OCEAN

SHEPPARD
So's exploding magma.
MCKAY
I am walking as fast as I can.

INT—DRILLING PLATFORM CHAMBER

[Weir enters. Teyla is already there, staring at the queen.]

WEIR
(desperate, but firm)
How do we disarm the self-destruct? You won't survive the blast any more than we will.

[The queen sighs.]

TEYLA
I can get the information we need.
WRATH QUEEN
If you were strong enough, you would have done so already. I can feel how weak you are.
TEYLA
We shall see who is stronger.

[Teyla goes into a meditative trance. The queen sighs, trying to resist.]

INT—HIVE SHIP

[Sheppard and McKay take off their suits.]

SHEPPARD
That took too long.
MCKAY
Yeah, well, I probably won't be able to disarm the self-destruct in time anyway, so we're really not in a rush.
SHEPPARD
Why don't we find it first, and then you can be negative?
MCKAY
You know, this ship is remarkably preserved.
SHEPPARD
You'd think after 10,000 years, she'd want to redecorate.
MCKAY
I'm serious. Hull damage is minimal. They probably just lost their main drive.
SHEPPARD
You're saying this thing is salvageable?
MCKAY
Well, I'd have to assess damage to the primary systems, but it's not completely out of the question. Of course, you'd probably just go and blow it up or slam it into a hive ship or something, and all that work would be for nothing.
SHEPPARD
Tick, tick, tick…

[McKay checks his sensor.]

MCKAY
This way.

INT—DRILLING PLATFORM CHAMBER

[Teyla continues to breathe deeply. Suddenly, she gasps.]

WEIR
Teyla?

[Teyla throws her head back with a cry and clutches her head.]

WRAITH QUEEN
You are weakening.
WEIR
Teyla, I think you should stop.
TEYLA
My concentration slipped for just a moment.
WRAITH QUEEN
And in that moment, I could have crushed you with a single thought. Perhaps next time, I will.
WEIR
There isn't going to be a next time.
TEYLA
Elizabeth, I am fine.
WEIR
No, you're not. I can see what this is doing to you. Let's just give McKay a chance.

INT—HIVE SHIP BRIDGE

[The alarm pulses.]

SHEPPARD
There it is.
MCKAY
Hmm.
SHEPPARD
What?
MCKAY
Well, I'm pretty sure this is the device, and I'm pretty sure it says we have less than half an hour.
SHEPPARD
So, turn it off.

[McKay tries to work some controls.]

MCKAY
(puzzled)
Hmm.
SHEPPARD
What does that mean?
MCKAY
It's not accepting commands of any kind. There must be some kind of a-a command code that needs to be entered first.
SHEPPARD
Can you figure it out?
MCKAY
(sarcastic)
Yes, well, command codes are usually quite simple, like the number "one" or the letter "A," like, in Wraith, which would be, um—
SHEPPARD
(interrupting)
Can you figure out the code or not?
MCKAY
No! Not if I stood here and tried for a million years, and we have, uh, (checking his watch)
…just under a million years less than that.
SHEPPARD
(into radio)
Elizabeth, you there?
WEIR
(into radio)
Any luck?
SHEPPARD
(into radio)
Negative. We don't get her command code in less than 30 minutes, we're dead.

EXT—DRILLING PLATFORM

INT—DRILLING STATION CHAMBER

[Still strapped to the gurney, the queen overhears Weir and Teyla speaking in the hall.]

TEYLA
This is the only way.
WEIR
I told you, Rodney has another idea.
TEYLA
That may or may not succeed. There is only one way to be certain.

INT—ATLANTIS CORRIDOR

WEIR
She said she would kill you next time.
TEYLA
I will not let that happen.
WEIR
No. We're going to give them more time.

[Weir leaves. Teyla watches her go with a cunning expression, then turns back to reenter the room where they're holding the queen.]

INT—DRILLING STATION CHAMBER

TEYLA
Ronon. There's been an emergency in the control room. Dr. Weir needs your help.
RONON
(indicating the queen)
What about her?

[Teyla unholsters her sidearm.]

TEYLA
I will watch the prisoner. Hurry.
RONON
Watch yourself.

[He leaves. Teyla lowers her gun.]

WRAITH QUEEN
Are you fool enough to try again?
TEYLA
I have very little to lose.

[The queen sneers as Teyla goes into a meditative trance. The queen gasps, trying to resist. Suddenly Teyla gasps and her eyes open. The queen opens her eyes and watches Teyla. Teyla blankly and turns to the bindings and undoes the straps holding the queen down. Once free, the queen raises her feeding arm to strike, then pauses, her eyes widening.]

WRAITH QUEEN
You are trying to hide something from me.

[Teyla stares in fear and shock at the queen, breathing hard.]

FLASHBACK

INT—ATLANTIS CORRIDOR

[Weir and Sheppard approach Teyla.]

WEIR
There's been a development.
TEYLA
You've been able to disarm the self-destruct device?
SHEPPARD
No, but, uh, Rodney thinks if he can tie in a naquadah generator, he can still get the Wraith cruiser to fly.
TEYLA
After all these years?
SHEPPARD
Yeah, the damage was superficial. The problem was the engines. McKay's already restored power, and I think…I can fly the ship to a safe distance.
TEYLA
That's a suicide mission.
SHEPPARD
Well, if I can ditch the ship on the mainland, maybe I can get far enough away from the explosion. I've got to go now.
TEYLA
Wait! Let me try again. If I can get her to give up her command code, you won't have to risk it.
SHEPPARD
You're in no condition to do that. Sorry.

[He leaves.]

END FLASHBACK

[The queen smiles, releasing her mental hold on Teyla, who faints. The queen strides down the hall]

EXT—ATLANTIS OCEAN

[The queen swims from the drilling platform back to her ship.]

INT—HIVE SHIP

[Sheppard stands by the self-destruct console. Hearing something behind him, he turns. The queen stalks towards him.]

SHEPPARD
Well. You're a hell of a swimmer, I'll give you that.

[He backs away from the self-destruct nervously as the queen approaches.]

WRAITH QUEEN
You have restored power?
SHEPPARD
(casually)
It'll fly.

[The queen reaches the controls and types in the code for the self-destruct abort. It shuts down.]

WRAITH QUEEN
You shall be rewarded…with a quick death!

[She is shot from behind by McKay, who fires his P-90 at the automatic setting. The queen stays standing and turns, stalking angrily towards McKay.]

MCKAY
(panicking)
She's not dying according to plan here!

[He starts to get away. Sheppard pulls his hidden pistol out and shoots the queen in the back several times. She finally falls.]

SHEPPARD
I thought you'd forgotten about me.
MCKAY
Of course not. I just had to wait for her to disable the device. Hey, just be thankful she didn't feed on you before she entered the command code.
SHEPPARD
Oh, that's why you didn't want to be bait.
MCKAY
No, no, no, no, you had to be bait, because she was expecting you to be the one trying to fly the ship.

[Sheppard seems to consider this logic while McKay nervously studies the queen.]

MCKAY
She is dead, right?
SHEPPARD
Let's get out of here.

EXT—DRILLING PLATFORM

INT—DRILLING PLATFORM CREW QUARTERS

[Teyla and Weir sit on a bunk. Sheppard heads to a different one and sits down.]

SHEPPARD
How are you feeling?
TEYLA
Oh, much better, now that I no longer feel the constant presence of a Wraith.
SHEPPARD
That was no ordinary Wraith. It took a lot of bullets to take her down.
RONON
I wish I was there.

[Ronon unbuckles his gun holster and gets into another bunk.]

WEIR
He's right, you know. I mean, deceiving a Wraith queen? That's no small feat.
TEYLA
(humble)
Well, when there are so many lives at stake, it's easy to summon the strength.

[McKay enters.]

MCKAY
(pleased)
Well, Zelenka's finally got communications back up. Turns out Atlantis had sent another jumper. It should be here within the hour.
WEIR
That's good news.
MCKAY
Hmm.
RONON
Yeah, no kidding. I can't wait to see the sun.

[He settles face down on his bunk.]

MCKAY
What? No, no, no. We haven't finished what we came here for. Look, Wraith's gone. We've got a ton of research to do.

[Weir grabs her jacket and stands.]

WEIR
Yes, we do.
MCKAY
In fact, I'm pretty sure we should be able to come up with a way to get auxiliary power online in a couple of days.

[He and Weir leave, Weir throwing a sidelong glance back at Teyla, who smiles.]

RONON
Is he serious?
SHEPPARD
Yeah, he is.
TEYLA
Well, I for one intend to spend the next little while…resting.

[She fluffs a pillow.]

RONON
Same here.
SHEPPARD
Yeah. Same here. Oh…

[Sheppard lies down.]

FADE OUT

END CREDITS
