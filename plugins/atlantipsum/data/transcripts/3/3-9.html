SGA 3.09 "Phantoms" Episode Guide

Précis

The Atlantis team is stranded on a mysterious planet, where the effects of an abandoned Wraith experiment threaten to turn them against one another.

Guide | Transcript

Transcript

EXT—ATLANTIS, DAY

INT—ATLANTIS CORRIDOR

[Sheppard and McKay walk with Weir towards the Control Room.]

SHEPPARD
Elizabeth, we may have a situation. Major Leonard's team? They're late returning from M1B-129.
WEIR
We sent them to check out an unusual energy reading, correct?
MCKAY
Mmm. An anomalous spike the MALP picked up on a 'gate scout. The planet is uninhabited, but I thought it warranted a closer look.
SHEPPARD
The point is, they missed a check-in. They're now six hours overdue.
MCKAY
We tried contacting them by the radio, but, uh, signal is intermittent at best. It's just a lot of static. It could have something to do with the anomaly, but I'm not sure.
SHEPPARD
I want to take our team, go see what happened.
WEIR
Of course. Take an additional marine unit as well and…the good Doctor Beckett, just in case.
SHEPPARD AND MCKAY
Right.

SHEPPARD
(into radio)
Major Leonard, this is Sheppard, come in.

[The others wince and take out their earpieces from the feedback Sheppard's transmission caused.]

SHEPPARD
(into radio)
Major Leonard?

[He pulls out his own earpiece.]

SHEPPARD
These things are useless.

[McKay studies his handheld computer.]

(annoyed)

(sarcastic)
Charming.

Hmm.

Bullet wounds, dozens of them.

Over here.

BECKETT
Also shot.

[Sheppard toes the other with his boot.]

SHEPPARD

[Ronon has found another body further in the bushes. Its skeletal hand still clutches a knife.]

I saw something.

It's probably nothing.

SHEPPARD
(to Teyla)
All right. We'll check it out. You and Ronon check out the immediate area around the cave. Keep an eye out for our friends, all right?

What is this?

Just like the energy conduit aboard a Wraith ship…and every bit of disgusting.

Possibly.

(warily)
Rodney?

Huh, we got power.

What's this, now?

Well, this is it. Ground zero for the energy readings.

This thing's still active?
MCKAY
Apparently so.
BARASSO
Actively doing what, exactly?

[McKay works his way through the tendrils to the center.]

MCKAY
It's some sort of…EM generator. It's emitting a modulating ultra-low frequency pulse. These, uh, energy conduits are acting almost like antennae.
BECKETT
Is that what's interfering with the radio communications?
MCKAY
Uh, probably. The Genii must've found this place, activated it somehow, and they couldn't figure out how to turn it off. So, they did whatever any other good "soldier" would do in their situation.
SHEPPARD
Shot the hell out of it.
MCKAY
Hmm.

Looks like it's healing itself.

Ah, it's the beauty of hybrid organic design.

(nervously)
Th-this frequency. Can it hurt us?

Mmm. It's doubtful. Apart from the obvious sterility issues.

Wha-what?!
SHEPPARD
He's kidding…you're kidding, right?
MCKAY
(noncommittally)
Eh.

[Teyla runs into the chamber.]

TEYLA
Colonel! We've found the team.

EXT—FOREST, M1B-129, DAY

[The rescue party stand around the bodies of three of the missing team.]

SHEPPARD
Okay, so…where's Major Leonard?
RONON
I don't know.
TEYLA
We were unable to locate his body.
BECKETT
They were killed by MP-5 fire. All three of them. Shot multiple times.
MCKAY
Hey! A camera!

[He picks up a small camcorder.]

MCKAY
Look at this.

[McKay opens the viewscreen and plays back the recording.]

VIDEO

[On it appears Leonard's team walking through the forest of M1B-129. The rearmost team member holds the camera.]

LARGENT
(narrating over video)
Okay, it's a road leading from the Stargate…kinda wish there was a burger stand or something around here. I'm starving.

[The camera abruptly switches scenes with a burst of static. The new image shows the team finding the Genii soldiers Sheppard's team encountered in the woods.]

LEONARD
(on videotape)
More of them.

[The camera switches scenes again. Now it records the cave, panning the central chamber and across the Genii bodies.]

LARGENT
Oh, god! More bodies in here!

[The camera scene switches again. They're still in the cave, but doing a more close up picture of the skeleton.]

LARGENT
What is this place?

[A burst of automatic gunfire sounds from outside.]

LARGENT
Who's that?

[He and his teammate run outside. Largent still holds the camera as he runs.]

[The camera switches scenes again. This time, it appears the camera was turned on accidentally. The picture is from hip-height. Largent and one of his teammates run through the woods towards the Stargate.]

LARGENT
We're not going to make it!

[His teammate gets struck from behind by a bullet and falls, reflexively firing his own weapon as he hits the ground.]

LARGENT
(frightened)
Prometheus, this is Largent! Where the hell are you!

[He continues running, then falls wounded himself, shot from behind. The camera falls on the ground, capturing the image of Major Leonard running past, shooting ahead of him.]

END VIDEO

[McKay switches off the camera.]

MCKAY
(perplexed)
He was calling for the Prometheus. That ship was destroyed last year.
TEYLA
(incredulous)
Major Leonard killed his own men? Why would he do that?
SHEPPARD
I don't know.
RONON
Maybe whatever caused the Genii to turn on each other did the same thing to our people.
BECKETT
That bloody machine back there?
MCKAY
I'll check it out.
SHEPPARD
No! We need to head back to Atlantis.
MCKAY
What? No. We can't leave. Look, the generator's still running.
SHEPPARD
I know.
MCKAY
Look, I have to at least figure out its purpose. They're probably right. It may somehow be linked to all of this.
SHEPPARD
Well, then we'll come back. But first things first, we have to transport our dead back to Atlantis. Then we'll return with more units and search for Major Leonard.

EXT—FOREST, M1B-129, DAY

[The team walk through the woods. Sheppard leads. Beckett and Mckay carry one field-made stretcher. Two of the marines carry the second. Ronon helps the third marine with the last body. Teyla brings up the rear of the procession.]

MCKAY
Can we stop for a second?
SHEPPARD
No.
MCKAY
(grunting with effort)
Look, I mean no disrespect, but this guy's heavy, and ever since I was shot in the ass by an arrow, I've been prone to sciatica…No?

[Ronon spots a misty object moving quickly through the woods, like when the Wraith try to confuse their prey.]

RONON
(hissing)
Down, down down!

[He lowers the stretcher to the ground, crouching. The others follow suit. Ronon raises his pistol. Sheppard runs down the line to Ronon.]

SHEPPARD
(low)
What is it?

[Both he and Ronon look carefully, but spot nothing.]

SHEPPARD
You okay?

[Ronon grimaces, holstering his weapon.]

RONON
Yeah.

[Sheppard signals to keep moving to the Stargate as he resumes his place at the front.]

EXT—STARGATE AREA, M1B-129, DAY

[The group reaches the Stargate, lowering their stretchers to the ground.]

SHEPPARD
Sergeant, dial the 'gate.

[The marine heads to the DHD. As soon as he presses the first glyph, the DHD explodes, the repercussion rocking the whole area, knocking everyone off their feet. Sheppard regains his equilibrium first, looking towards the marine sergeant who was closest to the blast, and is lying on the ground, unmoving.]

SHEPPARD
Carson!
BECKETT
Right!

[He struggles to his feet, patting Barasso on the arm as he passes.]

BECKETT
Get my medical kit!

[Sheppard checks the sergeant's neck for a pulse.]

SHEPPARD
He's dead.

[As Barasso reaches for Beckett's kit, he's shot by automatic weapons fire. He falls with a cry. Everyone takes cover.]

RONON
Wraith!

[He fires back at the assailant. Sheppard pulls Barasso to better cover.]

SHEPPARD
It's not Wraith! It's automatic gunfire!

[He spots Leonard firing on them from a distance.]

SHEPPARD
Leonard! Stand down! It's Sheppard!

[Leonard falls back. McKay reaches Beckett and a wounded Kagan, covering them with his pistol. Beckett takes the opportunity to scramble for his pack. Ronon gives chase to Leonard. Kagan clutches his abdomen with bloody hands, grunting with pain.]

KAGAN
How bad is it?
BECKETT
You'll be fine, son. Just lie back.
SHEPPARD
Leonard!

[Ronon paces the perimeter, clearly annoyed.]

RONON
(frustrated)
He's gone!

[Teyla treats Barasso. Beckett places a pressure bandage on Kagan's wound while Kagan writhes in agony.]

BECKETT
(soothing)
Easy, easy. You'll be all right. You'll be all right, son. Hey…

[He cradles Kagan's head in his arms and looks to Teyla and Barasso.]

TEYLA
(to Barasso)
Are you all right?
BARASSO
(in shock)
Yeah, I think so.

[McKay holsters his sidearm and heads over to the DHD. The inside is complete exposed, and all the control crystals are shattered to tiny pieces. Sheppard joins him.]

MCKAY
We are unbelievably screwed.

[Beckett continues to treat the two wounded soldiers. Ronon drags the dead sergeant to the other bodies.]

KAGAN
(panicking)
It's bad, isn't it? Am I going to die?
BECKETT
(determined)
Look at me. Look at me! Not if I have any say!
(looking over at Teyla and Barasso)
Teyla, keep pressure on the wound. We need to stop that bleeding!
(to Kagan)
Right.

[He continues treating Kagan who winces in pain.]

[McKay has his tablet computer connected to the vestiges of the DHD. Sheppard stands beside him.]

SHEPPARD
Is there any other way to dial?
MCKAY
(in disbelief)
Huh—
SHEPPARD
And don't say no if there is any possibility because I am not in the mood for any of your usual "impossible heroics" game.
MCKAY
(protesting)
I wouldn't do that!

[Sheppard glares. Ronon approaches.]

MCKAY
Look, no. Even if we could somehow manually dial, there's no power. Your pal Major Looney Tunes knew exactly where to set the charge.
RONON
We need to go after him.
MCKAY
No, what we need to do is go back to the bunker.
SHEPPARD
We're overdue. Elizabeth will contact us through the 'gate.
MCKAY
And she'll probably only get static, remember? Look, as long as that generator is emitting that pulse, we'll have little if any chance of responding to Atlantis if they radio. No, you want to get out of here? I need to shut that thing down.
SHEPPARD
(calling to everyone)
All right, we'll leave the dead here. We'll get 'em when we come back.

EXT—FOREST, DAY

[The group trudge back to the cave area. Beckett helps Barasso, keeping pressure on the bandage on his left shoulder. Ronon and McKay carry Kagan on a stretcher.]

KAGAN
I'm sorry about this, Doctor McKay.
MCKAY
(compassionately)
Hey, you don't worry about me. You just hang in there, okay?

INT—CAVE

[They've arrived at the cave. McKay and Teyla work in the central core.]

MCKAY
Teyla, you want to hold this?
TEYLA
Yes, of course.

[Barasso sits against a wall. Kagan lies on a table, Beckett working on him. Sheppard calls Beckett over out of hearing range.]

SHEPPARD
Doc? Are they going to be okay?
BECKETT
Barasso's shoulder's still bleeding internally, but I should be able to keep it in check. It's Lieutenant Kagan I'm worried about. He suffered severe penetrating abdominal trauma. He needs complicated surgery, blood transfusions, and all I've here is my medical kit.
SHEPPARD
All right, you do what you can to keep alive, okay? We're gonna get out of here soon.

BECKETT
Colonel…I'm afraid for Lieutenant Kagan, "soon" may only be a matter of minutes.

MCKAY

Glad to see you've got it all worked out.
MCKAY
(petulantly)
Definitely not all.
SHEPPARD
(impatient)
Some?
MCKAY
Actually, very little. Look, I can only surmise that it's some sort of Wraith…experiment.
TEYLA
(frustrated)
I am trying to impress upon Doctor McKay that determining how to turn the thing off is more pressing than learning what it actually does.
SHEPPARD
You don't even know what it does?
MCKAY
Well, I can now state for certain that it is in fact a generator.
SHEPPARD
We already knew that!
MCKAY
Look, the EM pulse it's emitting is-is very peculiar. It-it's fluctuating in-in systematic intervals along the ultra-low frequency.
TEYLA
Is that what made all these people kill each other?
MCKAY
I hope so.
SHEPPARD
You hope so?!
MCKAY
Otherwise, there are two bizarre things going on, and one is more than enough for me, thank you very much.

[Sheppard and Teyla look frustrated and worried.]

EXT—CAVE, DAY

[Ronon guards the cave entrance. Once again he spots the wispy movement. Alert, he heads towards it. He sees a Wraith running through the woods, getting to cover near the perimeter of weapon's range. He spots more running nearby.]

INT—CAVE

SHEPPARD
All right, whatever it is, I don't think it's doing what it's supposed to be doing. It doesn't make sense to have its prey kill each other before he can feed on them.
MCKAY
Uh, I think you're right. I don't think these "antennae" as I have so colorfully put it, have overgrown beyond their intended lengths. It's as if…whoever turned it on, dialed it up to eleven, and just left it there.

[He snaps off one of the tendrils.]

SHEPPARD
You don't think…
MCKAY
Yeah. The Genii. They must've discovered the place, started messing around with the machine without having the first clue as to what they were doing…which explains why the place is covered in this.

[He fingers a thick webbing of thin tendrils.]

TEYLA
And before they realized what they'd done…
MCKAY
They couldn't figure out how to turn it off, so they shot the thing, hoping they could somehow kill it, which only made it re-grow more, make it that much more difficult for someone with the ability to actually turn it off to actually turn it off.
SHEPPARD
So you can't…actually turn it off?
MCKAY
(defensively)
I never said that!
SHEPPARD
(impatient)
Well, what did you say?

[McKay huffs out a frustrated sigh.]

MCKAY
Look, the control console is shot to hell. As I was telling Teyla, I'll have to bypass it, isolate the primary actuator conduit, and follow it back to the core. Once I cut power to that—

[They hear Ronon's weapon being fired several times outside. All three of them run out of the cave.]

EXT—CAVE AREA, DAY

[Sheppard and Teyla, run out of the cave, weapons at ready. McKay follows. They don't spot anybody, including Ronon.]

SHEPPARD
Ronon!
TEYLA
Ronon!
SHEPPARD
Okay. We're gonna go after him. Rodney, you stay here and get that thing shut down.

[More distant weapons fire is heard over Teyla's left shoulder.]

TEYLA
This way.

[She and Sheppard head in the direction of the shots. McKay returns to the cave.]

EXT—ATLANTIS, DAY

INT—ATLANTIS CONTROL ROOM

[Weir enters the control room and walks up to a female technician.]

WEIR
Has Colonel Sheppard's team returned?
TECHNICIAN
Not yet.
WEIR
Huh. They're late. Have they dialed in a status update?
TECHNICIAN
We've received no communication from them since they left.

Okay, try to establish a radio link. Find out what's taking them so long. And I want a team standing by just in case they need assistance.
TECHNICIAN
Right away.

EXT—FOREST, DAY

[Teyla and Sheppard run through the forest. They pause at a tree trunk still smoldering from one of Ronon's blasts.]

SHEPPARD
We're heading in the right direction.
TEYLA
Do you think he's already beginning to feel the effects of the Wraith device?
SHEPPARD
Well, if he is, let's hope we find him before he finds us.

INT—CAVE CENTRAL CORE

[McKay works in the core. Beckett enters, holding a scalpel.]

BECKETT
You wanted a scalpel?
MCKAY
Yeah, I need to cut through this membrane. It's like surgery. It's more up your alley; maybe you ought to do it.
BECKETT
I'm a little preoccupied at the moment, thank you.
MCKAY
How is Kroger?
BECKETT
Kagan.
MCKAY
Kagan. Kagan. What is it with me and names?

INT—CAVE

[Beckett heads back to the outer chamber. He finds Barasso standing up by Kagan, struggling to strap on his vest and gun.]

BECKETT
What in the devil do you think you're doing?
BARASSO
Uhn. I'm fine.
BECKETT
You must certainly are not. Off your feet, now!
BARASSO
Uhn. Look. I'm okay, doc. We've got no one covering the entrance. I'm gonna take up watch outside.
BECKETT
We just stopped the bleeding!
BARASSO
Yeah, and if it starts again, I'll be right outside the door.
(he keeps speaking before Beckett can interrupt)
Sitting down, off my feet. I'll be able to keep better watch out there than I can in here.
BECKETT
(unhappy)
You don't go running off, you understand? The last thing I need is you bleeding to death in those woods.

[Barasso starts walking to the door, waving without turning around.]

BARASSO
I'm not going anywhere. Just look after Kagan!

[Beckett turns back to Kagan.]

BECKETT
Right. Okay.

[Beckett takes a clamp and checks the injury. Kagan jerks in pain, trying to keep his mind off things.]

KAGAN
How's Doctor McKay doing?
BECKETT
Good. He'll figure it out soon, have us out of here in a jiff. Smartest man I've ever met.

[He pulls out some bloody bandaging with his clamp and throws it away.]

BECKETT
Now…you're still bleeding. I need to go back in, apply additional ligatures, hopefully shut off the spigot once and for all. All right?

[He gives a reassuring smile.]

KAGAN
(breathlessly)
All right.

[Beckett claps Kagan on the shoulder than goes to his medical bag.]

EXT—FOREST, DAY

[Sheppard and Teyla run through the forest. They spot Ronon leaning against a tree, watching something in the distance. They stop and watch him warily.]

SHEPPARD
(low, to Teyla)
What's he looking at?

[Teyla peers through the foliage.]

TEYLA
I see nothing.
SHEPPARD
Ronon!

[Ronon glances over, raising his hand in warning.]

RONON
Stay back!

[Leonard fires his rifle through a gap in the trees, striking Teyla in the leg before she and Sheppard can take cover. She falls back with a strangled cry.]

SHEPPARD
Ronon! Cover fire!

[Sheppard goes to assist Teyla. Ronon glances over, then takes cover. He and Leonard exchange fire as Sheppard tries to get Teyla to safety. He lays her by a thick trunk, than crouches behind it.]

SHEPPARD
Leonard! Stand down!

[Leonard shoots at the tree. Sheppard looks annoyed as he dodges shrapnel. Ronon takes advantage of the distraction to get closer. From his point of view, Sheppard is exchanging fire with a Wraith. He shoots. The "Wraith" runs away. Sheppard sees Leonard take off, and Ronon giving chase.]

SHEPPARD
Ronon, stop! Ronon!
HOLLAND
Sheppard?

[Sheppard turns around. Where Teyla was lying he sees an airman in a tan Air Force jumpsuit. He is injured in the leg and has many contusions on his face.]

HOLLAND
(weakly)
What the hell are you doing here?
SHEPPARD
(stunned)
Holland?
TEYLA
(worried)
John? John?!

[Sheppard steps forward, recognizing Teyla, who's grimacing in pain. He reaches down to take her pulse at her wrist.]

TEYLA
Are you okay?
SHEPPARD
Yeah.

[He seems to snap out of his fugue state, looking at Teyla and reaching for bandaging in his vest.]

SHEPPARD
He got you pretty good. All right. I've got to stop the bleeding.

[He starts to bandage her leg. Teyla continues to look at Sheppard worriedly while biting back the pain.]

EXT—FOREST SHELTER, DAY

[Sheppard and Teyla move through the woods. Teyla limps on one leg, hanging onto Sheppard for support. They come to a makeshift shelter with foliage as camouflage and roof. Sheppard silently gestures with his chin. Teyla grabs onto a tree for balance and keeps watch. Sheppard cautiously moves ahead.]

SHEPPARD
(low, non-threatening)
Leonard?

[He pierces the curtain the shelter. It is empty, the remains of MRE packaging scattered inside. He hurries back to Teyla.]

SHEPPARD
We're clear.

[He helps her into the shelter. Once she's settled, Sheppard takes a more appraising look at the camp.]

SHEPPARD
Leonard must've built this place before he lost it.

[He comes back over to Teyla, looking at her bandaging. He reaches to adjust it.]

SHEPPARD
(apologetically)
The dressing's a little sloppy.

[He checks the wound.]

SHEPPARD
It's still bleeding pretty badly. I'm gonna change it, and then, uh, go get Beckett.

[He looks at her, trying to be reassuring. Teyla continues to study him cautiously.]

TEYLA
John…what just happened out there?

[Sheppard looks away, fussing with the bandages.]

TEYLA
The look on your face? The confusion?
SHEPPARD
Nothing. Somebody just popped into mind, and…caught me off guard, eh?
TEYLA
Do you think it has anything to do with the Wraith generator?
SHEPPARD
Hell if I know. Just don't worry. I'm fine. No violent urges yet, huh? What about you?

[Teyla looks at him questioningly.]

SHEPPARD
Getting any ideas about killing me?
TEYLA
No.
SHEPPARD
That's good to hear. Anyway, if this is some…Wraith experiment gone haywire, maybe that part of your DNA will make you immune to it.

[They hear the sound of rifle fire nearby. Sheppard goes to the tarp curtain and peers out. Leonard fires at imaginary enemies. Sheppard moves forward to a tree, Teyla crawling to the edge of the shelter. Leonard's rifle magazine is empty, so he discards it and starts shooting with his pistol at random, nonexistent enemies. Eventually that gun runs out of ammunition as well. He looks at it and grunts in frustration. Sheppard rises as Leonard throws down his gun in disgust.]

SHEPPARD

Oh, you shot me!

[Sheppard winces before picking up his radio again.]

WEIR
And none of you experienced any after effects from this Wraith device?
SHEPPARD
(into radio)
Well, I mean, we're all pretty creeped out.
BECKETT
It was the most unsettling thing I've ever experienced.

[Sheppard tries to ignore Beckett.]

SHEPPARD
(keying radio)
But…we're gonna be fine.
WEIR
All right, we'll check in in a few hours. Weir out.

[Sheppard puts down his radio.]

MCKAY
(accusingly)
You shot me!
SHEPPARD
Yes, Rodney, I shot you! And I said I was sorry.
RONON
You shot me, too.
SHEPPARD
(upset)
I'm sorry for shooting everyone! Just…the Daedalus will be here in a little while. Just…get some rest.

[Sheppard sits next to Teyla with a sigh.]

TEYLA
So…did you manage to get Captain Holland to safety?

[A host of emotions flickers over Sheppard's face.]

SHEPPARD
No.
TEYLA
(sighing)
Well…we survived.

[Sheppard nods, still looking guilty.]

SHEPPARD
Yes. We did.
MCKAY
I can't believe you shot me!

[Ronon laughs.]

SHEPPARD
(with long-suffering air)
Get some sleep, Rodney.

FADE OUT

END CREDITS
